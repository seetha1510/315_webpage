About me
Hi, I'm Seetha

Hello! I’m a Junior Computer Science major with a pre-medical track at Texas A&M. I’m passionate about service and giving back. When I’m not programming or learning complex biochemical pathways, I’m usually planning logistics for FLASH (Freshmen Leaders Advancing in Service and Honor), an freshmen leadership organization I have been involved in for three years and am currently serving as the Service Executive for this organization. I was a Big in the organization last year and I loved spending time with my freshmen and helping them settle into campus life. More importantly, it is fun to work with the other amazing staff members to host events all year long.

I’m from Chennai, India and moved to Texas during the junior year of high school. So, I like to think I have a good mix of Texan and Indian culture. Personal aspects of my life that I enjoy a lot are my hobby to stitch, annoy my dog and play badminton with my friends. I love reading novels and short stories on the internet. Another part of life I love is travelling, especially with my friends! I have been to multiple countries with my family, but travelling to Denver with friends is my favorite trip so far.

My mix in interest in the medical field and computer science is because I want to work in the health field to help those in need. I want to work in the intersection between computer science and the medical field - the health informatics sector. I want to have a close understanding of the field I’m working in and therefore I am also pursuing a medical degree.
